Team Cohesion

Understanding how people think, feel, learn, and communicate allows employers fit employees to teams, understand what individual members require to be efficient, and ensure that meaningful work is distributed accordingly.
A cheap and time-effective way to receive this information during the formative days of team creation is through personality tests such as Myers-Briggs or learning style tests.

Phronesis Collective have a diverse range of scores personality test scores. There is an even mixture of assertiveness, leadership/followership, intuitiveness, and creativity.
As individuals we learn, think and handle stress differently.
We are from diverse backgrounds and regions throughout Australia that have come together to achieve a common goal.
Should conflict resolution techniques be required, we should look to adapt our communication style to best suit the team member.
We can obtain clues on how to achieve this through the personality test results provided.

While group diversity is a benefit in creative thinking and problem solving, conflict is bound to arise.
Understanding how the team members of Phronesis Collective think and interpret information in useful in warding off conflict and getting the most from all individuals and the team.
Additionally, having a solid understanding how the individual thinks and feels will prepare them to contribute confidently to a group and seek the best from others.

There are many similarities and differences that can be seen in our desired jobs and associated career plans.
Soft skills such as excellent communication, ability to work under pressure, attention to detail, teamwork and problem-solving skills feature heavily through the IT industry.
While each role requires some knowledge of programming and computing experience, they are all different in their individual requirements.

Some of the skills our group required are also in high demand, such as SQL, NoSQL, Javascript, while a few of our required skills are not in high demand, probably because these skills are prerequisite skills we yet to attain.
This also outlined skill gaps in the industry and is worthwhile for our group to consider developing and attaining.
