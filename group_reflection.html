Individual Reflections

Towards the end of a project, it is important for team members to examine their individual thoughts and feelings and to identify their strengths and weaknesses. Phronesis Collective took a moment away from the group work to individually reflect on how well we worked as a group and provide their own perspective of the experience.

Profile

SGT Peta-Maree BUBEAR

Phronesis Collective Team Leader

Canberra ACT, Ngunnawal Country

What went well?

Scheduling two meetings a week worked well as it ensured that all team members were able to attend. Having an easy-to-read agenda allowed all the important points to be met and for the conversation to flow.

What could be improved?

Open, honest communication can always be improved upon. Members need to be accountable for absences and advise if they are planning to be away from group work. Alternately, emergencies arise, they should check in once practicable. Members should also practice being out of their comfort zone and try new things or seek guidance and support from their team members when they are unsure.

What is at least one thing that was surprising?

How well we worked as a team, especially around potential areas of conflict. On occasion some members misinterpreted the assignment brief. Instead of a dispute arising, all members were given the opportunity to provide their interpretation and a collective decision was made.

What is at least one thing that you have learned about groups?

To expect the unexpected and always have a contingency plan in place to redistribute workload etc, in the event of an emergency with a team member.

Group Reflection

It was agreed by all Phronesis Collective members that, as a collective, we were committed to the assignment, actively contributed to meetings, and readily took on tasks and responsibilities.

Initially, Microsoft Teams was a challenge for some members, but with support and encouragement from the experienced team members, became comfortable and active users.

GitHub, CSS and HTML was another area that some members struggled with. Fortunately, there was a strong mentor in the group who took actively lead of website creation and methodically updated it as information was provided. He made himself available for questions and patiently talked members though their mistakes and concerns in the live environment.

Many members considered there was areas for improvement regarding open and honest communication. As the team became more established, trust began to form. We anticipate that this trust will continue to grow as the assignments progress.

Conflict was often mitigated through discussion on the topic. Occasionally, individual members misinterpreted the assignment brief. After group discussion and consideration, an outcome was agreed to, and we moved on. As members consistently contributed to meetings and to the group chat, it allowed for opinions and ideas to flow and creativity to take hold.

Phronesis Collective Git repository audit trail accurately reflects the progress made with the report. The website was developed parallel to the written report element. This can be demonstrated in the audit trail. However, it is not a true representation of individual members contributions or performance towards the assignment. While each member contributed something to the repository, it should be noted that some members require further practice and development in this space. Much of the website development was finalised by the website mentor, with other members contributing based on their experience and comfort levels.
